What is the issue?

In New Zealand the new government is planning to remove and replace the current guidelines for relationship and sexuality education. With the change in government, New Zealand First promised in their election manifesto to “Refocus the curriculum on academic achievement and not ideology, including the removal and replacement of the gender, sexuality, and relationship-based education guidelines.” This means adequate education on gender and sexuality will not be provided and this could affect those in the LGBTQIA+ community.

Current RSE Policy

The current policies related to relationship and sexuality education were released in 2020. The Ministry of Health describes these policies as ​​"focusing strongly on consensual, healthy and respectful relationships as being essential to student wellbeing". Though 2018 research figured out that most schools have gaps in their sex education, especially around consent and relationships. Current policies state that a child’s parent or guardian is allowed to withdraw their child from sex education. The current RSE policies teach students a range of topics including reproductive systems, gender, sexuality, contraception, and much more. RSE is taught through primary to high school and ranges from less serious topics to more serious topics to fit an age-appropriate education.
